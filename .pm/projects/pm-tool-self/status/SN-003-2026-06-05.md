# Status note — 2026-06-05

## Highlights (this week)
- Pre-projects now work like real projects for planning people and meetings (T-0198): their stakeholders are full contacts with an email drawn from the shared address book, they can plan and hold their own kickoff/scoping meetings, and those meetings move across automatically when a pre-project becomes a real project.
- Junk tickets can finally be removed (T-0226): an admin can permanently delete a throwaway ticket, with a confirmation step. We used it to clear three leftover test tickets.
- The notes humans read are now written in plain language (T-0231): every agent write-up covers what we did, why, what would have happened if we did nothing, and the benefit — no jargon.
- We ran an independent review before shipping the pre-project work; it caught a command-line bug the normal build can't detect, plus a handful of smaller issues, all fixed before release.

## Next two weeks
- Three small usability fixes are queued in the current sprint: going back to and viewing earlier project phases (T-0227), bigger fonts and clearer icons (T-0228), and making ticket rows clickable in the backlog and sprint views (T-0229).
- Add a way for agents to write status notes (T-0232) — found while writing this one; today only people can, through the website or command line.

## Risks
- None new. The tool keeps growing; we keep shipping one small slice at a time and reviewing before release.

## Issues
- A few small gaps parked for later: making notification settings validate the same way across all three interfaces, and extending "remove the junk" to test sprints (only tickets can be deleted today).

## Milestone progress
- Hit: "Pre-projects reach parity with projects" (MS-010) — stakeholders with email, their own meetings, and meeting carry-over on convert are all live.
