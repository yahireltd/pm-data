## How the parts relate to milestones (structure walkthrough)

Written to clear up how the pieces of this project map together.

### The ladder
- **Project** — the whole Stock Management initiative.
- **Phase** — a big chunk of the journey, tackled roughly in order (we have 5).
- **Milestone** — the *finish line* for a phase: the outcome you can point at and call "done/live." Each phase here has exactly **one** milestone.
- **Sprint** — a ~2-week batch of scheduled work that *delivers toward* a milestone.
- **Ticket** — one concrete piece of work (feature / bug / spike).

So: **Project → Phases → each phase has a Milestone → Sprints chip away at it → Tickets are the actual work.**

### Phase ↔ milestone (1:1)
| Phase | Milestone (finish line) | Status |
|---|---|---|
| PH-001 Quick wins | MS-001 Stock quick wins shipped | **Sprint 1 in progress** |
| PH-002 Buffer Stock & Stock Levels | MS-002 Buffer stock & shortage alerts live | planned |
| PH-003 Quality Analytics & Decisions | MS-003 Quality analytics & decisions | planned |
| PH-004 Order Management | MS-004 Consolidated order management | planned |
| PH-005 Later / Parked | MS-005 Parked — needs scoping | planned |

### The one thing that reads as confusing (by design)
**Phase 1 "Quick wins" is not a theme — it's "the small, easy slice of *everything*, done first."** So Sprint 1 deliberately holds a **Quality** ticket (grading + photos) and an **Orders** ticket (view-all-orders), even though Quality and Orders each also have their own later phase.

The rule we applied:
- **Small, well-defined slice → Quick Wins (Phase 1) now** — e.g. "View all orders" button (T-0557, MS-001).
- **Big, needs-design version → its themed phase, usually as a spike** — e.g. full consolidated Orders + decision board (T-0558 spike, Phase 4).

Same for Buffer: the tickets that need the design call with Sandor (T-0559 upcoming-page split, T-0560 buffer page) sit in **Phase 2 / MS-002**, not in Quick Wins, because they're blocked on that decision.

Nothing is mis-filed thematically — Phase 1 is simply cross-cutting on purpose.
