# The breakthrough workshop

*This 29 Apr full-day session, facilitated by Axel Ferreyrolles (Humanness), is where the sales programme crystallised — the frame, the language, the priorities and the first prototype with named owners all date from this day. Held weeks before this pre-project record was formalised; recorded here retrospectively because everything since builds on it. Full slide summary: "Yahire Sales Workshop Summary Apr2026" (Humanness).*

## The frame it gave us

- **Sales Engine** (structure): the operating model — 10 nodes: opportunity flow, sales operations, IT/tools, roles & responsibilities, performance visibility, commission & incentives, communication, training & enablement, governance, customer segmentation & account management.
- **Sales Franchise** (leadership practice): the repeatable way Yahire leads, manages, reviews and develops sales performance — manager roles, expectations, dashboard, operating rhythm, feedback loops, people development, coaching, accountability without fear.
- **The core tension to honour**: Yahire's magic is intuition, trust, tribe spirit and commercial instinct. The engine is "the chocolate's outer crust — keeping the melty magic core safe", NOT a replacement for it. Twin risks named: becoming too process-driven / losing human quality as structure increases.

## What the room surfaced (Interview Mirror)

Foundational issues: lack of cohesion, direction + vision, enablement. Consequences: weak ownership, weak feedback, lack of clarity. Amplifiers: weak handovers, poor visibility, structural constraints. **Visibility was the #1 circled issue** — with customer handling, handovers, ownership rules, feedback/comms and consistent tools/systems close behind.

## What needs protecting (DNA)

Strengths: deep customer knowledge, quality across sales/service/logistics/products, human relationships and trust, tribe spirit, intuition + innovation, success through happy clients. Under threat: tribe spirit weakening, two-office split, "one team" becoming "two teams", individualistic behaviours potentially encouraged by commission logic, balance between system logic and intuition.

## Focus areas chosen for the engine

1. Governance · 2. Sales Operations · 3. Training & Enablement · 4. Communication

## Reference frameworks introduced

SAP demand-to-close / lead-to-opportunity models (phases with entry/exit criteria), RACI role grids, and sales-manager cadence calendars — Nathan and Sam each drafted their own cadence live on whiteboards.
